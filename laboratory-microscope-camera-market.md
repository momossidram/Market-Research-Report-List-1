<p><ul><li>CCD</li><li>CMOS</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The laboratory microscope camera market primarily consists of two types: CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) cameras. CCD cameras are known for their high sensitivity and superior image quality, making them suitable for low-light applications, while CMOS cameras tend to be more cost-effective, compact, and energy-efficient, offering faster image processing speeds. Both types cater to various scientific and medical applications, enhancing research capabilities by capturing detailed images and facilitating advanced analysis in laboratory settings.</p></p>
