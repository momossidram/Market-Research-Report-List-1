<p><p>The cough suppressant drugs market is primarily divided into two categories based on the type of cough: dry cough and wet cough. Dry cough suppressants, often containing agents like dextromethorphan, aim to inhibit the cough reflex, providing relief from a persistent, non-productive cough. Wet cough suppressants, on the other hand, may include expectorants that help thin mucus, making it easier to expel. Both types cater to different respiratory symptoms, shaping their market dynamics and consumer preferences.</p></p>
