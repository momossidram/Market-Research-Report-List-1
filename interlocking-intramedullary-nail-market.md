<p><p>An Interlocking Intramedullary Nail is a medical device used to stabilize fractures, particularly in long bones like the femur and tibia. It involves inserting a metal nail into the medullary cavity of the bone, secured with locking screws to enhance stability and promote healing. This technique is favored for its minimally invasive nature, which allows for quicker recovery times and reduced complication rates compared to traditional methods.</p><p>The Interlocking Intramedullary Nail Market is projected to grow significantly, with an expected CAGR of 14.7% during the forecast period.
