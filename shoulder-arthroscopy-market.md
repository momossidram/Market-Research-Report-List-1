<p><p>Shoulder arthroscopy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure used to diagnose and treat various shoulder joint conditions. It involves inserting a small camera, or arthroscope, into the shoulder joint through small incisions, allowing surgeons to view and address issues such as rotator cuff tears, shoulder instability, and impingement. The advantages of shoulder arthroscopy include reduced recovery time, less postoperative pain, and smaller scars compared to traditional open surgery.</p><p>The Shoulder Arthroscopy Market is experiencing significant growth driven by increasing incidence of shoulder disorders, rising awareness about minimally invasive surgical techniques, and advancements in arthroscopy devices. The demand for arthroscopic procedures is also supported by the aging population, which is more susceptible to shoulder-related injuries and conditions.
