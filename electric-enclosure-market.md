<p><p>The electric enclosure market is divided into two primary types: metallic and non-metallic. Metallic enclosures, typically made from steel or aluminum, offer robust protection against physical hazards and are ideal for high-temperature environments. They provide better grounding and electromagnetic interference shielding. Non-metallic enclosures, often crafted from fiberglass or plastic, are lighter, corrosion-resistant, and suitable for environments where moisture or chemicals are present. Both types serve crucial roles in safeguarding electrical components and ensuring operational efficiency in various industries.</p></p>
