<p><p>Polycrystalline Silicon Carbide (SiC) substrates are categorized into three main types: α-SiC, β-SiC, and others. α-SiC, known for its hexagonal crystal structure, exhibits superior thermal stability and mechanical properties, making it suitable for high-temperature applications. β-SiC, with a cubic structure, offers higher carrier mobility and is commonly used in electronic devices. Other types may include variants with tailored properties for specific applications. Together, these substrates are crucial for advancements in electronics, optoelectronics, and power devices.</p></p>
