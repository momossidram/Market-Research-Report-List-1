<p><p>The non-residential occupancy sensors market includes various types designed to detect presence and optimize energy use. PIR (Passive Infrared) sensors measure heat emitted by occupants, making them effective in static environments. Ultrasonic sensors utilize sound waves to detect movement, suitable for spaces with obstacles. Dual-technology occupancy sensors combine both PIR and ultrasonic technologies, enhancing reliability by reducing false triggers. Other types may include photoelectric sensors and microwave sensors, catering to diverse applications and requirements in commercial settings.</p></p>
